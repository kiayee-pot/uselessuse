Editorial Open Call — No, thanks


We welcome pitches and submissions for essays, interviews, fiction, poetry, and other unconventional textual formats which engage with notions of refusal and uselessness as strategic and liberatory ways of learning and living.

  • What is worth refusing?
  • What forms of uselessness not only free us from dominant narratives of being productive, useful, usable, but set us on radically and differently meaningful paths?
  • How does the notion of uselessness intersect with ecological and/or communal ways of being in the world?
  • What can we learn from the useless or vestigial?

We also invite engagements with refuse in the sense of trash, garbage, waste.

  • What are unexpected ways of engaging with waste?
  • How does life make waste of us, and how can life be reimagined from the POV of garbage?
  • How can we understand processes of decay, rotting, and fermentation within the context of education and learning?

Consider the above no more than suggestive prompts that you are welcome to go beyond. More than anything else, we are interested in writing which revitalizes uselessness and garbage as meaningful dimensions of life. Even better if your perspective not only unfolds within the content, but also suffuses the form or format of your piece.

+ Period

This Open Call will be open from January to June 2026, with pitches and submissions considered on a rolling basis.

+ Length

We do not have a word count range and will consider this on a case-by-case basis. However, we ask, regardless of the length of your piece, that your pitch or submission has rigour, that is: a powerful commitment to its interest or central throughline, whether that lies in seriousness, or disruption, or lucidity, or depth, or play.

+ Format

Please submit in both Word Doc and PDF formats. We do not accept submissions in Pages format.

We have no preference between British or American spelling. However, we enjoy the Oxford comma.

+ Process

All accepted pieces will undergo an editorial process that spans 1-3 rounds of editing in total, depending on the needs of the piece of writing.

+ Payment

We pay within the range of SGD50 to SGD400 per piece, depending on length, rigour, and genre. Our decision on the amount is final.

+ How to Submit

Pitches should include

  • a max. 200-word brief of what your piece is about;
  • a max. 120-word bio and why you are the right person for this piece of writing;
  • an estimated word count; and
  • 2-3 samples of your writing

Submissions should include

  • your submission in .docx and .pdf formats; and
  • a max. 120-word bio
